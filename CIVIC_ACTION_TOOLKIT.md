### How to Find Your Representative and Senator

1. Go to **https://legislature.vermont.gov/people**
2. Enter your town/city or ZIP code
3. View your district(s):
   - **Representative(s)** — You have one or two reps depending on district size
   - **Senator(s)** — Senate districts span multiple towns; may be 1-3 senators per district

### Email Format for Vermont Legislators

Standard format: **firstname.lastname@leg.state.vt.us**

Examples:
- Rep. Lucy Rogers (Lamoille-3): lrogers@leg.state.vt.us
- Sen. Becca Balint: bbalint@leg.state.vt.us

(Verify exact spelling via legislature.vermont.gov before sending)

| Time Window | Recommended | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Tuesday-Thursday mornings | Excellent | Legislators most often in Montpelier |
| Friday afternoons | Low | Many depart state house |
| Evening hours | Moderate | Use for voicemail only |

## PHONE CALL SCRIPT TEMPLATES

### Standard Bill Advocacy Script (Under 1 Minute)

~~~
[When operator answers:]
"Good morning/afternoon, my name is [YOUR NAME] and I'm a constituent
from [YOUR TOWN/CITY]. I'd like to speak with Senator/Representative
[LEGISLATOR LASTNAME] about [BILL NUMBER - e.g., S.298]."

[If legislator available:]
"Thank you. I'm calling as a resident of [TOWN] to urge you to
[SUPPORT/OPPOSE] [BILL NUMBER] because [ONE SENTENCE PERSONAL IMPACT].

Brief personal context: [2-3 sentences describing how this legislation
affects you, your family, or your community].

Three key points:
1. Economic/health benefit: [brief statement]
2. Community support: [mention any local orgs backing bill]
3. Urgency: [mention upcoming vote deadline if applicable]

Please let me know if you will [record your support/opposition on record].
Thank you for your time."

[If speaking to staffer:]
Same script, but ask: "Could you please pass this message directly to
the senator/representative? May I follow up with an email?"
End with: "Thank you, I appreciate your work on this."
~~~

### Opposing Legislation Script

~~~
[Opening same as above]
"I'm calling to express concern about [BILL NUMBER]. As a constituent
from [TOWN], I believe this bill would negatively impact our community
because [specific reason—cost, access, rights, etc.].

My specific worry is [personal example or data point]. I respectfully
urge you to vote NO / oppose advancing this legislation.

May I count on your opposition? Thank you for listening."
~~~

### Emergency Vote Alert Script (Use When Vote Imminent)

~~~
"This is an urgent call about [BILL NUMBER] which has a floor vote
EXPECTED [DATE/TIME]. As a constituent from [TOWN], I urgently request
you to [support/oppose] this bill before it reaches final passage.

This impacts [brief description—who or what affected]. Please act today.

Can you confirm your position? Thank you."
~~~

## FOLLOW-UP PROCEDURES

### After Initial Contact

| Step | Timeline | Method |
|------|----------|--------|
| Send initial message | Day 1 | Email + optional phone call |
| Wait for acknowledgment | Days 2-7 | Legislative offices reply within 1 week |
| Follow-up reminder | Day 7+ | Phone call referencing prior email |
| Escalate to chair/staff director | Day 14+ | Request meeting if bill is priority |
| Mobilize fellow constituents | Ongoing | Organize group calls or joint letters |

### If You Receive No Response

1. **Call again** — Speak to different staff member
2. **Copy the committee chair** — CC them on follow-up emails
3. **Request position statement** — Ask office to clarify stance publicly
4. **Share on social media** — Tag legislator handle and post update politely
5. **Mobilize peer network** — Have others in same district reach out simultaneously

## BEST PRACTICES REMINDERS

- Always include full name + street address + phone number (constituent verification required)
- Mention town/city early in all communications
- Keep scripts under 1 minute verbally; under 250 words in emails
- Personalize every outreach — even small tweaks increase response rate
- Thank legislators for previous support where accurate ("thank you for supporting minimum wage increase")
- Avoid threats or insults — even disagreement sounds stronger without hostility
- Reference pending vote dates to create urgency
- CC allies sparingly; mass-email blasts reduce perceived legitimacy
- Log your own contacts for tracking and reporting back to DSA chapter

## EMERGENCY ALERT PROTOCOL

When a bill moves unexpectedly fast through chamber:

1. **Notify chapter within 1 hour** of receiving alert
2. **Activate SMS/text tree** using Signal or WhatsApp groups
3. **Send standardized template** with clear ask + deadline timestamp
4. **Set target call volume** (e.g., 50 calls/day x 5 days = 250 total)
5. **Assign coordinator** to tally incoming responses and report back to leadership
6. **Prepare press release** if public visibility helps amplify pressure
