Funded via ~7% payroll tax (split between employers/employees). Implementation targeted for January 1, 2027.
- **Status**: Pending/governor's signature as of late May 2026.

**ACTION ITEMS:**

### Housing for All

**BILLS FOUND:**

- **H.565**: Tighten security deposit rules (effective July 1, 2026) — security deposits now fully refundable

- **NOT PASSED**: Statewide rent control remained absent; Burlington maintains municipal ordinance only

**OPPORTUNITY AREAS:**

- Universal statewide rent control does NOT exist
- H.772 (controversial rollback of tenant protections) was defeated after strong opposition from legal-aid groups

**ACTION ITEMS:**

- Push for statewide rent control legislation in next session
- Expand VHCB funding allocations beyond $10M
- Support tenant organizing around Burlington model replication

### College for All

**BILLS FOUND:**

**ACTION ITEMS:**

- Monitor funding sustainability
- Extend to full 4-year programs vs. 2-year limitation

**BILLS FOUND:**

- Status: Introduced/advanced in 2026 session

- Connect with health-service providers for screening program rollout

- Automatic enrollment for qualified Vermonters

### Green New Deal

**BILLS FOUND:**

- **Global Warming Solutions Act (GWSA)**: Mandates 26% emissions cut by 2025, 40% by 2030, 80% by 2050 (relative to baseline years)
- Previous "Vermont Green New Deal" proposal (2020) did not pass but established policy groundwork

**ACTION ITEMS:**

- Expand Climate Superfund collection mechanisms

### Union Power

- **S.128**: Reduces exemptions from minimum wage/overtime regulations (extends coverage to more workers)
- Minimum wage increased to $14.42/hour effective Jan 1, 2026 (up from $14.01)

**ACTION ITEMS:**

- Campaign for Proposal 3 passage in November 2026 election

### Popular Vote for President — CORRECTED STATUS

**VERMONT HAS ALREADY ENACTED THE NATIONAL POPULAR VOTE INTERSTATE COMPACT**

| Metric | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Vermont NPVIC Status** | **ENACTED — April 22, 2011** |
| **Bill Number** | S.31 |
| **Senate Vote** | Passed 20-10 on February 23, 2011 |
| **House Vote** | Passed 84-51 on April 15, 2011 |
| **Signed By** | Governor Peter Shumlin |
| **Vermont Was** | 8th state to enact the compact |
| **Vermont Electoral Votes** | 3 EVs |
| **National Total Enacted** | 222 EVs (41.3% of Electoral College) |
| **Threshold Needed** | 270 EVs (majority needed to activate compact) |
| **Gap Remaining** | 48 electoral votes needed |

**CONTEXT:**

The National Popular Vote Interstate Compact (NPVIC) is an agreement among states to award all their electoral votes to whichever presidential ticket wins the overall popular vote nationwide. The compact goes into effect only when states representing 270 electoral votes have enacted matching legislation.

Vermont enacted NPVIC into law on April 22, 2011, when Governor Peter Shumlin signed S.31. Vermont was the 8th jurisdiction to join the compact. Vermont's 3 electoral votes are already counted in the national total of 222 enacted EVs.

**ACTION ITEMS (SHIFTED FROM ADOPTION TO DEFENSE AND EXPANSION):**

- **Defend Vermont's NPVIC membership** — monitor for any repeal attempts in future sessions
- **Educate constituents** that Vermont has already taken this step — many residents may be unaware
- **Encourage neighboring states** (New Hampshire, Maine already enacted) to build regional momentum
- **Advocate for federal legislation** supporting national popular vote as complementary to the compact
- **Highlight Vermont's early leadership** on this issue as part of broader democratic reform narrative

**OPPORTUNITY AREAS:**

- No further state-level NPVIC action needed — Vermont is already a member
- Focus energy on persuading non-member states with larger EV counts (e.g., Michigan 15 EVs, Pennsylvania 19 EVs, Texas 40 EVs)
