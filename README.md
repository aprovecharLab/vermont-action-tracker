## 🏛️ About This Tracker

### Purpose

This repo documents Vermont state legislative activity aligned with the published platforms of four significant Social Democracy political organizations: the **Democratic Socialists of America (DSA)**, **Social Democrats of America (SDA)**, the **Socialist International (SI)**, and **Social Democrats USA (SDUSA)**. Rather than tracking legislation through the lens of a single organization's priorities alone, this tracker synthesizes the shared and overlapping commitments across all four to provide a broader, more inclusive picture of how Vermont legislation measures up to the social democratic tradition as a whole.

The program pillars below are generalized from the published platforms of all four organizations and reflect areas of substantive convergence:

1. **Thriving Working Class Communities** — Universal healthcare (Medicare for All / publicly funded healthcare), housing as a right, affordable and free education at all levels, ending mass incarceration and the war on drugs, robust family support (paid leave, universal childcare), and reproductive freedom
2. **An Economy for the Working Class** — Living wages and reduced work hours, progressive and wealth-based taxation, Green New Deal / climate justice through economic transformation, strong collective-bargaining and union rights, public or social ownership of key resources, and community-wealth building
3. **Working-Class Foreign Policy** — International solidarity and anti-imperialism, humane immigration policy and freedom of movement, demilitarization and reduction of military budgets, support for Palestinian self-determination, and an end to economic warfare
4. **Working-Class Democracy** — Universal suffrage and voting rights protection, electoral reform (popular vote, proportional representation), reducing concentrated institutional power (Supreme Court reform, ending money in politics), and expanding participatory democracy

Each organization emphasizes these pillars differently — DSA frames them through class struggle and systemic transformation; SDA emphasizes reformist socialism within the Democratic Party; SI brings a global, human-rights-centered perspective; SDUSA advocates incremental, mixed-economy social democracy with multilateral foreign policy. Full platform summaries for each organization are provided in the reference section below.

### Methodology

- Research conducted primarily via **LegiScan VT**, official legislature.vt.gov database, press coverage from VTDigger, Seven Days, and local outlets
- Bills categorized under relevant Social Democracy organization program pillars regardless of sponsor party affiliation
- Bills are tagged with any combination of DSA, SDA, SI, and/or SDUSA alignment where their content intersects with that organization's published platform
- "Opportunity Gaps" flag areas where no active legislation matches progressive demands across any of the four organizations
- Every month includes concrete next-step recommendations for organizers

### Limitations

- Does not replace formal constituent advocacy (still contact legislators directly!)
- Federal-state jurisdiction boundaries mean some topics are excluded (military budget, constitutional amendments requiring Congress)
- Speed depends on researcher bandwidth—updates may lag during intensive organizing periods
- Alignment assessments reflect published platforms only, not formal endorsements or organizational positions on specific Vermont legislation

## 📖 Social Democracy Organizations Referenced

The following organizations are referenced in alignment analyses throughout this tracker. Platform summaries are drawn from each organization's published materials as of 2026.

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Description |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|
| **DSA** | Democratic Socialists of America | Largest socialist organization in the U.S.; its 2025-2026 program "Workers Deserve More" advocates for universal childcare, Medicare for All, free public college, a comprehensive Green New Deal, robust labor union empowerment, expanded paid family leave, voting rights protection, opposition to imperialist wars, support for Palestinian self-determination, a new democratic constitution with proportional representation, abolition of ICE, and the end of money in politics |
| **SDA** | Social Democrats of America | Socialist organization affiliated with the Socialist International (socialists.us); advocates for free education, universal healthcare, anti-death penalty policies, reproductive rights, universal basic income (UBI), the right to unionize, separation of church and state, and the belief that government should make life bearable for all and ensure citizens' liberties; positions itself as part of the Democratic Party's Socialist Caucus |
| **SI** | Socialist International | Worldwide federation of socialist, social democratic, and labor parties; its Declaration of Principles affirms democratic socialism grounded in universal human rights, gender equality, climate justice, social solidarity, and an internationally coordinated economy placed under democratic control to reduce poverty and inequality; calls for advanced participatory democracy, public ownership and democratic management of key productive resources, universal access to education, healthcare, and housing, workers' rights to organize, and a foreign policy based on peace, anti-imperialism, and solidarity among peoples |
| **SDUSA** | Social Democrats USA | Reformist social democratic organization; advocates for a mixed economy with a robust welfare state, universal publicly funded healthcare and education, a living-wage federal minimum wage, progressive taxation, expanded Social Security, affordable housing, strong collective-bargaining rights, climate action through a Green New Deal-type program, abolition of the death penalty, protection of reproductive rights, full LGBTQ and civil-rights equality, separation of church and state, and a foreign policy rooted in multilateralism, human rights, and continued NATO commitment |

> **Note:** This tracker examines policy overlaps with these organizations' published platforms, not formal endorsements or memberships. Bills are assessed based on their content alignment with stated organizational goals.
