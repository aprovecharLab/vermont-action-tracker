## 🏛️ About This Tracker

### Purpose

This repo documents Vermont state legislative activity aligned with **DSA Vermont's 2025-2026 program pillars**:

1. **Thriving Working Class Communities** (Healthcare, Housing, Criminal Justice, Education, Drug Policy, Family Support)
2. **An Economy for the Working Class** (Labor, Taxation, Climate, Unions)
3. **Working-Class Foreign Policy** (International Solidarity, Immigration, Peace)
4. **Working-Class Democracy** (Voting Rights, Electoral Reform, Constitutional Power)

### How This Repo Works Together

| Document | Purpose | Update Frequency |
|----------|---------|------------------|
| **Monthly Reports** (`/2026/`) | What happened in the legislature this month + what to do about it | Monthly |
| **[Civic Action Toolkit](./CIVIC_ACTION_TOOLKIT.md)** | Reusable scripts, templates, and contact guides for constituent engagement | Periodically (as needed) |
| **README** (this file) | Landing page, current month summary, archive index | Monthly |

The toolkit stays evergreen—it's built once and refined over time. Monthly reports reference specific toolkit sections so readers flow naturally from "here's what's happening" to "here's exactly how to respond."

### Methodology

- Research conducted primarily via **LegiScan VT**, official legislature.vt.gov database, press coverage from VTDigger, Seven Days, and local outlets
- Bills categorized under relevant DSA program headers regardless of sponsor party affiliation
- "Opportunity Gaps" flag areas where no active legislation matches progressive demands
- Every month includes concrete next-step recommendations for organizers

### Limitations

- Does not replace formal constituent advocacy (still contact legislators directly!)
- Federal-state jurisdiction boundaries mean some topics are excluded (military budget, constitutional amendments requiring Congress)
- Speed depends on researcher bandwidth—updates may lag during intensive organizing periods
